Scientific Importance:

Sagittarius A* (Sgr A*) is the supermassive black hole at the center of our Milky Way galaxy. It serves as a natural laboratory for studying extreme gravity, general relativity, and accretion physics. The stars orbiting Sgr A* have provided the most robust evidence for the existence of supermassive black holes.
